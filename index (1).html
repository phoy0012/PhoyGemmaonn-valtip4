About the Project

From plantation
to prosperity

Laos is a Least Developed Country with nearly 25% of its population below the poverty line. VALTIP4 works to transform plantation forestry and wood processing into key drivers of poverty alleviation, increased tree cover, and climate change mitigation.

Project Objectives

Six strategic goals
driving change

A multi disciplinary approach addressing policy, technology, markets, gender equity, and workplace safety across the forestry value chain.

01

Policy & Governance

Strengthen the enabling environment for plantation expansion. Support smallholders and MSMEs through improved policy, regulation, and industry frameworks.

02

WTRC Self Sufficiency

Build the Wood Technical Research Centre at NUoL into a fully self supporting, certified, fee for service testing and training facility.

03

Timber R&D

Address technical barriers in wood product recoveries, optimise rubberwood processing, and develop bio based adhesives and circular economy practices.

04

Markets & Supply Chains

Build market intelligence and forecasting of global and regional demand trends to identify export and domestic opportunities.

05

Gender & Social Inclusion

Examine the effects of industry development on female livelihoods, particularly for ethnic minority women, and improve equity across the supply chain.

06

Occupational Health & Safety

Establish routine industrial hygiene and OH&S protocols across forest and wood processing industries, addressing significant safety gaps.

Research Streams

Six streams,
zero shortcuts

A comprehensive multi disciplinary program spanning policy, technology, markets, social inclusion, and workplace safety.

  1. Policy, Governance & Industry

    Strengthening policy frameworks and institutional capacity for sustainable forest industry expansion.

    Dept. of Forestry & ANU

  2. WTRC at NUoL

    Building the Wood Technical Research Centre into a fully self supporting, certified testing and training facility.

    National University of Laos

  3. Timber R&D & Circular Economy

    Addressing technical barriers in wood product recoveries with focus on rubberwood, bio adhesives, and circular practices.

    Griffith & Melbourne

  4. Supply Chains & Markets

    Building market intelligence and forecasting of global and regional trends for Lao plantation resources.

    Salwood Asia Pacific

  5. Gender & Social Inclusion

    Examining gender effects of industry development to improve equity, with focus on ethnic minority women.

    NUoL Social Sciences

  6. Occupational Health & Safety

    Establishing routine OH&S protocols with exchange visits to Australian regulators and on site training.

    DPI Queensland

Project Phases

Five year
roadmap

  1. Year 1 . Jul 2025 to Jun 2026

    Inception & Baseline

    Establish project steering committee, recruit in country manager, conduct inception workshop, and develop full M&E framework with baseline data collection.

    OH&S Review Market Intel WTRC SWOT
  2. Year 2 . Jul 2026 to Jun 2027

    Research Build Up

    Scale up in country research, begin plantation resource characterisation studies focusing on rubberwood. Deliver first WTRC business model.

    Resource Reports WTRC Model Logistics
  3. Year 3 . Jul 2027 to Jun 2028

    Mid Term & Expansion

    Mid term review to assess progress. Begin EWP product testing and wood product technical trials. Intensify OH&S on site training.

    EWP Trials Bio Adhesives MSME Support
  4. Year 4 . Jul 2028 to Jun 2029

    Consolidation

    Consolidate research findings across all streams. Support WTRC self sustaining operations and deliver circular economy trial reports.

    Final Reports Circular Economy
  5. Year 5 . Jul 2029 to Aug 2030

    Completion & Handover

    Finalise all technical research. Complete the EWP demonstration building at NUoL. Confirm WTRC self sufficiency pathway. Final M&E evaluation.

    EWP Building Project Close

network
